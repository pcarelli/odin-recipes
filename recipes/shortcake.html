Strawberry Shortcake

Description

Strawberry shortcake is a light, summertime dessert that is usually comprised of strawberries, shortcake and fresh whipped cream. It's best enjoyed outdoors after a dip in the lake, or after a fresh grilled cheeseburger or hot dog.

Strawberry shortcake is the king of all shortcakes. You never hear about blueberry, raspberry or pineapple shortcakes, because they don't stand a chance against the superior strawberry. They also don't have the added benefit of alliteration, unless you're making a Star Fruit Shortcake, but where does one even acquire a Star Fruit??

Ingredients

  • 3 pints of fresh strawberries
  • 1/2 cup of white sugar
  • 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 4 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons of white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/3 cup of shortening
  • 1 egg
  • 2/3 cup of milk
  • 2 cups of whipped heavy cream

Steps

  1. Slice the strawberries, toss them with 1/2 cup of white sugar and set aside
  2. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F. Grease and flour one 8-inch round cake pan.
  3. In a medium bowl combine the flour, baking powder, 2 tablespoons white sugar and the salt. With a pastry blender, cut in the shortening until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Make a well in the center and add the beaten egg and milk.
  4. Stire the mixture until just combined
  5. Spread the batter into the prepared pan.
  6. Bake at 425 degrees F for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool partially in the pan or on a wire rack.
  7. Slice partially cooled cake in half, making two layers.
  8. Place half of the strawberries on one layer and top with the other layer. Top with remaining strawberries and cover with the whipped cream.
